03

Assignment 1 Proposal

My proposed creative tool for Assignment 1 is an interactive web-based expressive experience centred around a calm moving line that responds to emotional input. As users add feelings or thoughts, the line gradually changes in behaviour, becoming more unstable, layered, and disrupted over time. The project explores how emotion can be translated into abstract visual form rather than being represented literally.

Purpose

The purpose of this creative tool is to give users a simple but expressive way to externalise emotion through interaction. My current concept is an interactive web-based experience built around a calm moving line that reacts to emotional input. Users may be invited to enter a feeling, briefly explain what may have caused it, and adjust a general intensity slider from 1 to 10. Rather than turning these responses into direct storytelling or fixed categories, the tool translates emotional input into visual behaviour. As more responses are added, the line may begin to shift, branch, distort, or lose stability, allowing emotion to be represented through movement and change rather than literal description. I am interested in exploring how a minimal visual system can still communicate something deeply personal and layered. At this stage, the project remains intentionally open, as the exact form of interaction and visual response may continue to evolve. However, the overall aim is to create a reflective space where emotion can be experienced as rhythm, tension, build-up, and disruption in an abstract but engaging way.

Value

The value of this creative tool lies in its ability to make something internal feel visible, immediate, and interactive. Emotions are often difficult to explain clearly because they can be layered, unstable, and deeply personal. By using a responsive line as the main visual language, the project offers users a way to express what they are feeling without relying only on words. This gives the tool both emotional and creative value, as it encourages interpretation rather than forcing users into overly rigid forms of self-description. I think the abstraction is important, because it allows the experience to feel more open and personal. Instead of trying to define emotion too literally, the project suggests it through motion, instability, pacing, and visual disruption. There is also value in how the interaction may create a sense of accumulation over time, where each contribution leaves a trace and gradually shifts the overall system. The intensity slider adds another layer of flexibility, allowing users to define emotional intensity in their own way. Overall, the project aims to create an experience that feels thoughtful, expressive, and open to multiple interpretations.
